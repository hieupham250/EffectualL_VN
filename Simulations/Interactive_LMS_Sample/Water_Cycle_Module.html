The Water Cycle: Nature’s Recycling System

Module 1: Content

The water cycle is nature’s way of recycling water. It involves processes like evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection. Energy from the sun powers the cycle, moving water from oceans, rivers, and lakes into the atmosphere, forming clouds, and bringing it back as rain or snow.

  • Evaporation: Water turns into vapor and rises into the air.
  • Condensation: Water vapor cools and forms clouds.
  • Precipitation: Water falls back as rain, snow, or hail.
  • Collection: Water gathers in rivers, lakes, oceans, and soil.
