Understanding Earth's Layers

Our planet isn't just a solid ball; it's composed of several distinct layers, each with unique properties and roles in Earth's dynamics. These layers are the **crust**, the **mantle**, and the **core**.

The Crust

The Earth's crust is its outermost solid shell, relatively thin compared to the other layers. It's where we live and where all known life exists. There are two main types: oceanic crust (denser, thinner) and continental crust (less dense, thicker).

Plate Tectonics: Earth's Dynamic Engine

Plate tectonics is the unifying theory of Earth science, explaining the movement of Earth's lithosphere (crust and uppermost mantle) on the asthenosphere. This movement leads to earthquakes, volcanoes, and the formation of mountain ranges.
