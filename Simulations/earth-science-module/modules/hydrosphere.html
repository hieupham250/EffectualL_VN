The Hydrologic Cycle

Water on Earth is dynamic and continually in motion through the unending circulation known as the hydrologic cycle. This cycle involves the continuous exchange of water among the oceans, atmosphere, and continents. Processes like evaporation from surfaces, transpiration from plants, condensation to form clouds, precipitation falling back to Earth, infiltration into the ground, and runoff across the land all contribute to this cycle. This movement holds the key to distributing moisture across the planet.

Influence on Weather and Climate

Water, especially water vapor in the atmosphere, has a huge influence on our planet's weather and climate. Water vapor is the source of all clouds and precipitation. When water changes state, it absorbs or releases latent heat, which is a primary energy source that helps drive many storms and atmospheric processes. Ocean currents, driven by global winds that are themselves powered by unequal heating, play an important role in redistributing heat around the globe, influencing climate patterns. Climate patterns like El Niño and La Niña, which are related to the warming and cooling of Pacific Ocean waters, demonstrate how ocean conditions can significantly affect weather worldwide
